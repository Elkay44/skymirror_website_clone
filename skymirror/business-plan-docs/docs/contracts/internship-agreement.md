## 4. DUTIES & RESPONSIBILITIES

### 4.1 Primary Responsibilities

**Phase 1 (Months 1-2): Foundation**
- Complete onboarding and training program
- Learn Company codebase and development workflows
- Deliver 3-4 production features under mentor guidance
- Attend all lectures and complete learning assignments
- Participate in daily stand-ups and sprint ceremonies

**Phase 2 (Months 3-4): Mastery**
- Independently own and deliver 4-5 production features
- Conduct code reviews for peers
- Begin mentoring junior interns (5% time allocation)
- Contribute to technical documentation
- Participate in architecture discussions

**Phase 3 (Months 5-6): Leadership**
- Own complete product modules
- Teach 5+ Academy sessions (20% time allocation)
- Mentor 1-2 junior interns
- Deliver 3-4 features plus refactoring work
- Contribute to curriculum development

### 4.2 Professional Standards

The Intern agrees to:
- Follow all Company policies and procedures
- Adhere to coding standards and best practices
- Maintain professional communication and conduct
- Respect team members and contribute positively
- Protect confidential and proprietary information
- Report conflicts of interest immediately
- Comply with all applicable laws and regulations

### 4.3 Learning Commitments

The Intern commits to:
- Complete assigned lectures and learning materials
- Actively participate in 1:1 mentorship sessions
- Seek feedback and implement improvements
- Maintain personal development goals
- Share learnings with team members
- Contribute to knowledge base and documentation

## 5. TEACHING & MENTORING OPPORTUNITIES

### 5.1 Academy Teaching (Months 5-6)

**Opportunity:**  
High-performing interns will be invited to teach Academy sessions starting in Month 5.

**Compensation:**
- Per-session rate based on complexity and preparation (specified separately)
- Paid within 15 days of session delivery
- Additional compensation for curriculum development

**Requirements:**
- Minimum 4/5 performance rating in Month 4 evaluation
- Completion of "Teaching Fundamentals" training
- Approval from Program Lead and Academy Lead
- Preparation of teaching materials and exercises

**Time Allocation:**
- Maximum 20% of work time (8 hours/week)
- Sessions typically 2-4 hours each
- Preparation time included in allocation

### 5.2 Peer Mentoring (Months 3-6)

**Opportunity:**  
Interns may mentor junior interns starting in Month 3.

**Compensation:**
- Integrated into overall compensation structure
- Considered in performance evaluations
- Contributes to leadership development and career progression

**Responsibilities:**
- Weekly check-ins with mentee
- Code review support
- Answer technical questions
- Share experiences and learnings

The Intern acknowledges access to Confidential Information, including but not limited to:

- Business strategies and plans
- Customer data and information
- Financial information
- Product roadmaps
- Trade secrets and proprietary methods

### 7.2 Non-Disclosure Obligations

The Intern agrees to:
- Keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential
- Not disclose to any third party without written authorization
- Use Confidential Information only for internship duties
- Return or destroy all Confidential Information upon termination
- Not retain copies of any Confidential Information

### 7.3 Duration

These confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement and continue indefinitely, or until the information becomes publicly available through no fault of the Intern.

### 7.4 Exceptions

Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that:
- Is publicly available through no breach by Intern
- Was known to Intern before disclosure
- Is independently developed by Intern after internship
- Must be disclosed by law (with prior notice to Company)

## 8. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION

### 8.1 Evaluation Schedule

**Month 2:** Foundation Phase Review  
**Month 4:** Mid-Program Evaluation  
**Month 6:** Final Evaluation  
**Extension Period:** Quarterly evaluations (Months 9, 12)

### 8.2 Evaluation Criteria

Interns are evaluated on:
1. **Technical Skills (30%)** - Code quality, problem-solving, technical knowledge
2. **Productivity (25%)** - Task completion, velocity, time management
3. **Communication (20%)** - Collaboration, documentation, asking questions
4. **Learning & Growth (15%)** - Receptiveness to feedback, improvement
5. **Professionalism (10%)** - Reliability, attitude, team contribution

### 8.3 Rating Scale

**5 - Exceptional:** Exceeds all expectations  
**4 - Strong:** Exceeds most expectations  
**3 - Meets Expectations:** Satisfactory performance  
**2 - Needs Improvement:** Below expectations  
**1 - Unsatisfactory:** Significant concerns

### 8.4 Performance Improvement Plan

If performance falls below expectations (rating less than 3):
- Formal improvement plan created within 5 business days
- Specific goals and timeline defined
- Additional support and resources provided
- Weekly progress reviews
- Re-evaluation after 4 weeks

### 8.5 Impact on Compensation

Performance ratings directly impact:
- Quarterly bonus eligibility and amount
- Extension offer consideration
- Teaching opportunity eligibility
- Full-time employment consideration
- Recommendation strength

## 9. TERMINATION

### 9.1 Termination by Company

**With Cause:**  
The Company may terminate immediately for:
- Breach of this Agreement
- Violation of Company policies
- Misconduct or unprofessional behavior
- Poor performance (after improvement plan)
- Breach of confidentiality
- Criminal activity
- Misrepresentation in application

**Without Cause:**  
The Company may terminate with 2 weeks' written notice for any reason.

### 9.2 Termination by Intern

The Intern may terminate with 4 weeks' written notice.

**Early Termination:**  
If Intern terminates with less than 4 weeks' notice:
- Final stipend prorated to last day worked
- No quarterly bonus eligibility
- Equipment must be returned immediately
- Learning budget reimbursements may be reclaimed

### 9.3 Termination Procedures

Upon termination:
- All Company property returned within 5 business days
- Final stipend paid on next regular pay date
- Confidentiality obligations continue
- Exit interview conducted
- Access to systems revoked
- Final evaluation provided (if applicable)

### 9.4 Consequences of Termination

**For Cause Termination:**
- No severance or bonus
- No recommendation letter
- Potential legal action for damages

**Without Cause or Voluntary Termination:**
- Prorated stipend to last day
- Prorated quarterly bonus (if eligible)
- Recommendation letter (if performance satisfactory)
- Alumni network access (if in good standing)
