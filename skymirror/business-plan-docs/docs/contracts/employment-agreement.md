## 7. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 7.1 Confidential Information
"Confidential Information" includes:
- (a) Business plans and strategies
- (b) Financial information
- (c) Customer and supplier information
- (d) Technical information and source code
- (e) Employee information
- (f) Any information marked confidential

### 7.2 Obligations
The Employee shall:
- (a) Keep Confidential Information strictly confidential
- (b) Use Confidential Information only for Company business
- (c) Not disclose to any third party without authorization
- (d) Return all Confidential Information upon termination

### 7.3 Duration
Confidentiality obligations continue indefinitely after termination.

### 7.4 Exceptions
Obligations do not apply to information that:
- (a) Is publicly available through no fault of Employee
- (b) Is required to be disclosed by law

## 8. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 8.1 Work Product
All work product created by the Employee in the course of employment belongs to the Company, including:
- (a) Inventions and discoveries
- (b) Software and code
- (c) Designs and creative works
- (d) Documentation and materials

### 8.2 Assignment
The Employee hereby assigns to the Company all rights in work product created during employment.

### 8.3 Moral Rights
To the extent permitted by law, the Employee waives moral rights in work product.

### 8.4 Prior Inventions
The Employee has disclosed in Schedule B any prior inventions that should be excluded from this Agreement.

### 8.5 Cooperation
The Employee shall cooperate with the Company to protect intellectual property rights, including signing documents and providing testimony.

---

## 9. NON-COMPETITION

### 9.1 During Employment
During employment, the Employee shall not:
- (a) Work for a competitor
- (b) Start a competing business
- (c) Solicit Company customers or employees

### 9.2 Post-Employment Non-Compete
For [12] months after termination, the Employee shall not:
- (a) Work for a Competitor in a similar role
- (b) Start a business competing with the Company

"Competitor" means any business that provides:
- AI-powered attendance management solutions
- Learning management platforms
- Custom software development services

### 9.3 Geographic Scope
Non-compete applies in: [Hungary / European Union / Worldwide]

### 9.4 Compensation
During the non-compete period, the Company shall pay [50]% of the Employee's final base salary as compensation.

### 9.5 Waiver
The Company may waive the non-compete obligation in writing.

---

## 10. NON-SOLICITATION

### 10.1 Customers
For [12] months after termination, the Employee shall not solicit or do business with any customer or prospect with whom the Employee had contact during the last [24] months of employment.

### 10.2 Employees
For [12] months after termination, the Employee shall not recruit, solicit, or hire any Company employee.

---

## 11. TERMINATION

### 11.1 Notice Period
After the probationary period, either Party may terminate with:
- Less than 1 year service: [30] days' notice
- 1-3 years service: [45] days' notice
- More than 3 years service: [60] days' notice

### 11.2 Termination by Company for Cause
The Company may terminate immediately for:
- (a) Gross misconduct
- (b) Material breach of this Agreement
- (c) Fraud or dishonesty
- (d) Conviction of a criminal offense
- (e) Continued poor performance after warning

### 11.3 Termination by Employee
The Employee may terminate:
- (a) With notice as specified above
- (b) Immediately if Company materially breaches

### 11.4 Garden Leave
During the notice period, the Company may:
- (a) Require the Employee to remain away from work
- (b) Continue to pay salary and benefits
- (c) Restrict contact with customers and colleagues

### 11.5 Return of Property
Upon termination, the Employee shall immediately return:
- (a) All Company property and equipment
- (b) All documents and materials
- (c) All Confidential Information
- (d) Access credentials and keys

### 11.6 Exit Interview
The Employee agrees to participate in an exit interview.

## 12. DATA PROTECTION

### 12.1 Employee Data
The Company will process the Employee's personal data in accordance with:
- (a) GDPR and applicable data protection laws
- (b) The Company's Privacy Policy
- (c) Legitimate business purposes

### 12.2 Data Categories
The Company may process:
- (a) Contact and identification information
- (b) Employment and payroll information
- (c) Performance and evaluation data
- (d) IT usage data

### 12.3 Employee Rights
The Employee has the right to:
- (a) Access personal data
- (b) Correct inaccurate data
- (c) Request deletion (subject to legal requirements)
- (d) Object to certain processing

### 13.1 Governing Law

### 13.2 Dispute Resolution

- (a) Good faith discussion

- (c) Hungarian labor courts
